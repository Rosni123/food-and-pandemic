FOOD FOR THOUGHTS

FOOD AND PANDEMIC

As the COVID‐19 pandemic of 2020 unfolds, considerable attention has focused on the resiliency of food supply chains in a time of crisis. Food supply chains have needed to adjust rapidly to demand‐side shocks, including panic buying and changes in food purchasing patterns, as well as plan for any supply‐side disruptions due to potential labor shortages and disruptions to transportation and supply networks. The challenges are facing in food supply chains worldwide during the current crisis and our team will try to offers thoughts on policy and industry strategies to enhance food supply chain resilience. Sustainable Goals

1.Prioritization of Basic Needs

The wastage of production means the foods are not prioritized as per basic needs of people. When producers produce only the necessary nutritious food to sustain the population, cutting off extra demands that we had before (like halting to produce anything except balanced diet) we’ll have the ability to manage the best from what we have. Resource management is vital in eradicating the unbalanced food chain supply caused by pandemic outbreak.

2.Technological Maneuver in sustainable food supply chain management

Government and other private organization should focus in technological maneuver over traditional food chain supply. Government can create the proper environment for startups and private company to invest over traditional farming. When the technological innovations are applied in food-chain supply will not disrupt immediately. And, farmers should be used only for growing intensive labor crops. Farmers are key to food chain supply. So, keeping them immune also should be our top priority. For this proper guidelines and practices for safe social distancing should be taught and put into action.

3.Packaging and containment for sustainability

The major problem right now is wastage of food which is happening when foods are kept openly. Packaged foods are always certified to last longer than foods exposed freely. So, by packaging the foods and keeping them in proper containment we can eradicate wastage of tons of foods from the market. According to research from FAO, the virus can survive on a variety of surfaces such as plastic or cardboard used in packaging, but it is unlikely that this type of exposure would be sufficient to make a person sick. Similarly, foods will indirectly be free from viruses when it’s not exposed.
